# Lime Rock Mountain Circuit Layout

Track layout files and documentation.

--------------------OVERVIEW--------------------

Lime Rock Park is a natural-terrain road racing venue located in Lakeville, Connecticut, nestled in the state's northwest corner. The circuit is short, a mere 1.57 miles, and seemingly straightforward, with only one left turn, known fittingly as the "Left-Hander." At first glance the course seems to have more in common with a country lane winding through the foothills of the Berkshire Mountains than a purpose-built motorsports complex. Racers sometimes refer to its seven corners, six right-handers and one left, as a road racing short-track, due to the high average speeds and the fact that there is no opportunity to relax during a lap.

The track was born thanks to local racing enthusiast Jim Vaill, who often raced a friend's MG-TC in a gravel pit on Vaill family property. He was approached by members of the SCCA, who proposed building a permanent road racing course. Using an aerial survey of the 325-acre park, Vaill laid out a plan for the circuit, its shape largely dictated by nature - the surrounding woods and rolling hills that form a natural amphitheater. The circuit was designed by John Fitch, an engineer who had been the lead driver for Briggs Cunningham's team and who later raced a factory Mercedes-Benz 300 SLR. 

The first race day was held April 28, 1957, with a G-Production class race won by Ted Sprigg in an Alfa Romeo Giulietta and an MG class race won by Charles "Skip" Callanan in an MG-TC. It is now the nation's third oldest continuously operating road racing venue.

Lime Rock cemented its fame in 1959 when it hosted a historic Formula Libre where Roger Ward beat out a Maserati Formula One car and a DBR-1 Aston Martin while driving a Kurtis-Kraft Offy midget car — a result that shocked the racing world and helped break down the barriers between professional and amateur road racing in America.

Lime Rock Park has hosted most every major American road racing sanctioning body, including IMSA, ALMS, Grand-Am, NASCAR, SCCA, Trans Am, and World Challenge. Legends to have raced at Lime Rock include Mario Andretti, Dan Gurney, Paul Newman, Derek Bell, Tom Kristensen, and many others.

The track was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 2009. In the 2008 repaving and upgrade, the original track design and layout features were preserved entirely, meaning drivers today follow the exact same line with the same corner references as the first racers did in 1957. 

--------------------ALTERNATE HISTORY/LAYOUT--------------------

The mod I've created has been ported from the original rFactor and explores a proposed original layout for Lime Rock Park, that expands the short "bull ring" into the surrounding mountain and hillsides. Turning the 1.57 mile track into a flowy ~2.92 mile circuit, that goes past the original "Big Bend" turn 1 and up into a mountain, rejoining the original track between turns 2 and 3 on the classic course. The top speeds of the course are much higher due to this extension after turn 1 and turns this already high speed track into a true staple of North American racing. With the real-life loss of IMSA at the course and constant pressure from neighbors to limit racing due to noise, this mod brings a new potential to this classic racetrack and an idea of what could have been.
